Meet Google Gnome

The smart yard is finally here.

"Ok Gnome, what's the windspeed?"

Google Gnome is like having a tiny, little meteorologist at your beck and call. Ask Gnome all kinds of questions, like: "what’s the difference between cirrocumulus and cumulonimbus clouds", "why is humidity the worst", and, for windswept hair in summertime photo shoots, "which way is the wind blowing"?

“Ok Gnome, play fetch”

Google Gnome is designed to work hard and play hard, with entertainment for humans and dogs alike. It can play music in the garden, Marco Polo in the pool, and hide and seek in the yard. It can even play fetch by throwing squeaks thither and hither via 3-D sound, offering hours of fun in the sun.

“Ok Gnome,

mow the lawn”

Google Gnome comes programmed with the ability to take charge of your backyard tools. Go hands-free with your pressure washer, leaf blower, tiller, and more. Its cutting edge riding lawnmower integration features a proprietary autopilot mode, allowing you to kick back and ride shotgun on days when you just can’t even.

“Ok Gnome, set alarm to smell the roses”

Google Gnome manages yard tasks like a pro. When mosquitoes start biting, it can add bug spray to your shopping list. It can tell you how to prune your silver maple. It can even remind you to take time for yourself, because what better way to disconnect than to have your connected device alert you to literally stop and smell the roses?
